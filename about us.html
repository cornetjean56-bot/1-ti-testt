About Mauritius Athletics


After the introduction of the Sports Act in 1984, the Mauritius Sports Association was dissolved, giving rise to several independent federations, each governed by the rules of their respective international federations.

Originally known as the Mauritius Amateur Athletics Association since its inception, a name change was approved on April 5, 2001, and the association became the Mauritius Athletics Association (MAA).

Since its founding in 1952, the MAA has been affiliated with the IAAF, the global governing body for athletics. At the time, the AMA was affiliated with a commission of the Mauritius Sports Association.

On November 30, 1986, the AMA established its own rules and regulations, which were amended on December 14, 1987. The Mauritius Athletics Association operates independently from other sports federations, while remaining governed to this day by the laws of the Mauritius Olympic Committee and the IAAF.

The MAA headquarters has been located at the Maryse Justin Stadium in Réduit since 1993, in a building named after the late Primo Nebiolo, former president of the IAAF. As a structure recognized both regionally and internationally, the federation is responsible for organizing major competitions such as the Indian Ocean Island Games, African Championships, as well as international meetings and world-class marathons.
